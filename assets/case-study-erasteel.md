# Erasteel — Building a Transformation Office

**Role:** Head of Strategy Development
**Period:** August 2024 – Present
**Location:** Paris, France

---

## Context

Erasteel is a global specialty steel manufacturer producing high-speed steels and powder metallurgy alloys. When I joined, the business had ambitious EBITDA improvement targets but no structured mechanism to coordinate, track, or govern the initiatives needed to hit them.

Improvement work was happening in silos — commercial, operations, and supply chain each running their own workstreams without a shared view of dependencies, sequencing, or cumulative impact. Reporting to the board was a PowerPoint exercise that happened monthly, not a live decision tool.

The ask was to build Erasteel's first Transformation Office from scratch and lead it.

---

## Approach

### 1. Mapping the initiative landscape

The first step was turning scattered ideas into a structured portfolio. I worked across functions and geographies to surface, qualify, and size every live improvement initiative. Each was assessed on three dimensions: EBITDA potential, delivery confidence, and strategic fit.

This produced a portfolio of **€18M in EBITDA initiatives** with clear ownership, phasing, and interdependencies mapped across countries and functions.

### 2. Building the governance model

A transformation office is only as useful as its governance rhythm. I designed a lightweight operating model:

- **Weekly**: workstream leads report on blockers, milestone status, revised forecasts
- **Monthly**: executive steering with EBITDA bridge view showing planned vs. actuals
- **Quarterly**: portfolio rebalancing to reallocate effort as priorities shift

The goal was making governance a tool for decision-making, not a reporting ceremony.

### 3. Executive dashboards and KPI frameworks

I built the reporting infrastructure from scratch — executive dashboards showing initiative status, EBITDA bridge evolution, and KPI health across the portfolio. These replaced the monthly PowerPoint with a live, structured view that leadership could interrogate rather than just read.

### 4. Commercial Strategy 2026

In parallel, I led the research, analysis, and structuring of Erasteel's Commercial Strategy 2026 — shaping the company's long-term market positioning. This included competitive landscape analysis, customer segment prioritization, and identifying strategic growth levers.

The strategy also included driving market expansion into **Southeast Asia** and **Latin America**, identifying growth opportunities and building the analytical foundation for market entry decisions.

---

## Outcomes

- **€18M EBITDA initiative portfolio** structured, tracked, and governed across countries and functions
- First Transformation Office established and operational within 3 months of joining
- Executive dashboards and KPI frameworks deployed, replacing manual reporting
- Commercial Strategy 2026 delivered and presented to leadership
- Active market expansion roadmap developed for SEA and LATAM

---

## Learnings

**Governance eats strategy for breakfast.** The best initiative portfolio means nothing without a system that keeps it alive. The hardest part of this work was not the analysis — it was designing a governance rhythm that senior stakeholders would actually use and maintain.

**Start with sequencing, not sizing.** Early work focused too much on quantifying every initiative before locking in a portfolio view. In practice, sequencing (which initiatives unblock others, which are time-sensitive, which share resources) matters more than getting the EBITDA estimate right to the decimal.

**Build for the decision, not the slide.** Every dashboard and KPI framework I designed started from the question: what decision does this need to support? That framing consistently produced simpler, more used tools.
